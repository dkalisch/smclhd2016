SMC Local Hack Day 2016

at Saint Mary's College of California

December 3, 2016 in Moraga, CA

What is a Local Hack Day?

All graduate and undergraduate students are invited to participate in the first Local Hack Day organized by Saint Mary's College. Local Hack Day is a 12-hour hack day bringing student communities together to celebrate building technology.

On December 3rd, we will join other students around the world who will be simultaneously hosting their own Local Hack Days at their schools, resulting in the single largest day of student hacking ever. Throughout the day, there will be chatrooms and fun activities that will enable you to meet students in your local community as well as across the globe. Whether you're a seasoned hackathon veteran or have never been to a hackathon before, Local Hack Day provides a way to learn, build, and share with students from all over the world.

Lunch, refreshments (water, iced tea, coffee, tea), and dinner will be provided to registered attendees.

Keynote

Makeing Sense of Data

Topic

Big data is the latest buzz word. Enterprises are finally starting to grasp the value of big data analytics. Capturing and streaming all data isn't too hard; it's the searching and analysis where the real challenge lies. How can we make sense of the massive amounts of data? Where and what is the story? And how can we present the finding in a captivating way to leave an impression to the audience?

Workshops

HTML

The web has come so far that straight HTML by itself is impractical (incomplete and amateurish). We'll show you how to use the Skeleton framework to create a "responsive design."

Installing Ubuntu

There's a whole bunch of reasons to consider Ubuntu. To name a few, Ubuntu might be faster, safer, and cheaper than other mainstream operating systems such as Windows. In this workshop you can learn how to get started with Ubuntu.

Programming with Python

Python is a very flexible language. In this workshop we'll see how you can harvest Tweets with Python.

Introduction to Data Analysis with R

R is an invaluable tool for math and statistics, but it can be used in many other applications. In this workshop we will explore some methods of data analysis with R.

Dressing Up Web Pages

Web pages don't dress themselves. Learn how to make a webpage that viewers will appreciate.
